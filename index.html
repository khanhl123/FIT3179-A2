What the map shows

In 2021 there’s a clear east–west split: NSW and Victoria record the highest working-from-home shares after long lockdowns (Sydney ~31%, Melbourne ~26%), which tempers driving rates.

Queensland and Western Australia are more car-dependent, with over half of workers driving (Brisbane ~56%, Perth ~63%).

South Australia, Tasmania and the Northern Territory also lean toward driving, with comparatively modest WFH uptake.

Overall, private-car commuting dominates outside NSW/VIC, while WFH surges in Sydney and Melbourne reshape travel patterns there.

Shift in Transportation Modes (2016 → 2021)

Click a mode (legend or line) to highlight it. Others will dim. Double-click anywhere to reset.

What stands out

Commuting by car as a driver stayed dominant but fell from about 62% in 2016 to 53% in 2021.

The biggest shift was working from home, which jumped from ~5% to ~21%, reflecting pandemic-era changes.

Public transport contracted sharply—train dropped from ~7.2% to ~2.5%, bus from ~3.5% to ~1.8%, with tram/light rail also declining—while walking, cycling, and car-passenger all edged down.

Overall, the slope chart shows a clear move away from shared modes toward home-based work and, to a lesser extent, private car use.
